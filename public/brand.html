01Brand at a glance

SoCal Academy of Knowledge (SoCal AoK) is a prestigious, academically serious Islamic institution in Northridge, California. It teaches the Qur'an, the authentic Sunnah, and essential life and academic skills — from the Evening Maktab and Weekend School to vocational and youth programs, and its two differentiators: a full-time Hifz program and a K-12 Homeschool Academic Support program no other valley institution offers at this level.

Personality

Prestigious and academic, yet warm. Scholarly without being distant; trustworthy and rooted in tradition; welcoming to families of every background.

Brand pillars

  • Faith — grounded in the Qur'an and authentic Sunnah.
  • Knowledge — rigorous, structured learning (the lamp of noor over the open Qur'an).
  • Character — adab, integrity (Amanah), and excellence (Ihsan).
  • Community — a supportive Ummah for students and families.

Promise

Nurturing Faith & Knowledge.

Clear space & minimum size

  • Keep clear space on all sides at least equal to the height of the crescent in the mark.
  • Minimum size: full lockup no smaller than ~110px / 28mm wide; emblem favicon no smaller than 16px.

Misuse — never

  • Recolor outside the palette, add gradients/shadows/glow, or outline the mark.
  • Stretch, condense, or rotate it.
  • Place the color emblem on a busy photo without its navy field behind it.
  • Embed the Bismillah or Qur'anic text inside the compact logo or favicon.

05Typography

The official typefaces are Playfair Display (display) and Inter (body). Both are royalty-free under the SIL Open Font License and already load on socalaok.org.

Playfair Display

Display & headings · weights 600–800. Page titles, section heads, the wordmark feel.

Inter

Body & UI · weights 400–600. Body copy, navigation, forms, captions.

Arabic: دار العلم and the Bismillah are set in Amiri. Keep hierarchy clear: one display weight per screen, generous line spacing, and never set long body copy in the serif.

06Motifs & iconography

Four motifs carry the brand. Use them deliberately and sparingly.

  • Mihrab arch — structure, tradition, sacred space; the frame of the crest.
  • Crescent & stars — Islamic identity; the night of learning and reflection.
  • Open Qur'an — knowledge at the center of everything the academy does.
  • Noor (light) — guidance and enlightenment, shown as descending rays.

A geometric eight-point star pattern may be used as a subtle background accent in gold or green at low opacity. Avoid clip-art or photographic icons.

07Imagery & photography

  • Warm, natural light; authentic moments of students and teachers learning — not staged stock.
  • Respect modesty norms and the privacy of minors; obtain consent before publishing any child's image.
  • Favor compositions that harmonize with navy, green, and gold; avoid clashing or oversaturated tones.

08Voice & tone

Prestigious yet warm; scholarly but accessible; confident without boasting. Lead with the benefit to the child and family, and use Islamic terminology correctly and respectfully.

Do
  • “A structured Hifz path guided by qualified, caring instructors.”
  • “Nurturing faith, character, and academic excellence.”
  • Address parents' real questions plainly.
Don't
  • “The best Islamic school in California, guaranteed.”
  • Jargon, hype, or fear-based messaging.
  • Overuse exclamation points or buzzwords.

09Islamic standards & adab

﷽
  • Bismillah placement: use the Bismillah as a header/banner element on the website and letterhead, set with the Unicode ligature (U+FDFD) in Amiri — never baked into the compact logo or favicon.
  • Adab with sacred text: Qur'anic text must be legible, never stretched, cropped, or placed on floor graphics or low-traffic merchandise.
  • Credibility: present teacher credentials and ijazah where possible — authentic scholarship is core to the brand.
  • Arabic name: the academy's Arabic name is دار العلم (Dar al-ʼIlm). It appears on the website header and letterhead in Amiri — not inside the compact logo, which stays English-only.
